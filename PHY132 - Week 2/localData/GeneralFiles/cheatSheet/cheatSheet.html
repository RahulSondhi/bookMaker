1. Calibration: normal if initial 1.0 mV signal is 10 small boxes tall
2. Rate
a. 300—150—100—75—60—50 rule
b. Count number of beats in 10 second rhythm strip and multiply x6
3. Rhythm
Sinus rhythm if:
a. Every P wave is followed by a QRS
b. Every QRS is preceded by a P wave
c. P wave is upright in leads I and II

5. Mean QRS Axis
a. Lead I positive, Lead II positive → Normal Axis
b. Lead I positive, Lead II negative → Left axis deviation
c. Lead I negative, Lead II positive → Right axis deviation
d. Lead I negative, Lead II negative
i. aVF positive → Right Axis Deviation
ii. aVF negative → indeterminate axis
6. P wave abnormalities
a. Check leads II and V1 for left/right atrial enlargement
7. QRS wave abnormalities
a. LVH
i. R wave in aVL > 11mm tall
ii. Height of R wave in lead aVL + depth of S wave in lead V3 > 20mm
(female), 28mm (male)
b. RVH
i. R>S in lead V1
ii. R wave>7mm tall in lead V1
c. RBBB
i. Lead V1 – rSR’ pattern where R’>r (bunny ears)
ii. Lead V6 – R/S ratio > 1
d. LBBB
i. V1 – small R wave (rS) OR QS pattern with SHARP downward S wave (vampire
teeth)
ii. V6 – monomorphic R wave with no S wave
e. Pathologic Q waves
8. ST Segment/T wave abnormalities
a. ST elevations/depressions
b. T-wave inversions
c. Peaked/hyperacute T-waves
d. U waves
